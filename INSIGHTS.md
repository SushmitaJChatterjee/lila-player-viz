# INSIGHTS.md — What I learned from the Player Journey Tool

---

## Insight 1: The Storm is the #1 killer across all maps — by a huge margin

**What I noticed:**
Across all 5 days of data, storm deaths outnumber player-vs-player kills by roughly 8 to 1.

**The numbers:**
- AmbroseValley: 17 storm deaths vs 2 player kills
- Lockdown: 17 storm deaths vs 0 player kills
- GrandRift: 5 storm deaths vs 1 player kill

**What this means:**
Players are not extracting in time — the storm is catching them before 
they can fight each other or escape. This suggests the storm timing may 
be too aggressive, or players don't have enough warning/time to react.

**Actionable items:**
- Add an earlier visual/audio storm warning to give players more time
- Review storm speed and shrink timing on all 3 maps
- Consider adding more extraction points so players aren't forced to 
  travel too far under storm pressure

**Why a Level Designer should care:**
If players are dying to the storm and not to each other, the game feels 
like a timer race — not an exciting extraction shooter. PvP encounters 
are what make the game fun. The map design should create opportunities 
for fights BEFORE the storm closes in.

## Insight 2: GrandRift has a consistent death cluster near Mine Pit

**What I noticed:**
The heatmap for KilledByStorm on GrandRift shows a concentrated hotspot 
directly near the "Mine Pit" area — it appears consistently across 
multiple days of data.

**The numbers:**
- Mine Pit area accounts for the highest concentration of storm deaths 
  on GrandRift
- This pattern repeats across Feb 10-14, suggesting it's not random

**What this means:**
Something about Mine Pit's position on the map makes it a death trap. 
Either the storm consistently closes through this area, players get 
caught looting there too long, or there's no clear escape route.

**Actionable items:**
- Review the storm path — does it consistently pass through Mine Pit?
- Add a clear escape route or extraction point near Mine Pit
- Consider adding a visual cue in-game warning players that Mine Pit 
  is a high-risk zone when the storm is active

**Why a Level Designer should care:**
Consistent death clusters in specific named areas signal a map design 
problem. Players may be learning to avoid Mine Pit entirely, making 
that area of the map "dead" content that nobody visits.

## Insight 3: Lockdown has zero PvP kills — the storm ends matches before fights begin

**What I noticed:**
Lockdown is the close-quarters map — it should naturally produce the 
most player-vs-player fights. Instead, the data shows 17 storm deaths 
and literally 0 kills by other players.

**The numbers:**
- KilledByStorm: 17
- Killed by another player: 0
- This means 100% of deaths on Lockdown are storm-related

**What this means:**
The storm is ending matches before players even encounter each other. 
The map's close-quarters design is wasted if nobody survives long 
enough to fight. Players are spending their entire match running from 
the storm instead of engaging.

**Actionable items:**
- Slow down the storm speed specifically on Lockdown
- Shrink the initial map size so players spawn closer together 
  and encounter each other earlier
- Add more central loot to encourage players to stay in the middle 
  of the map longer rather than running to edges

**Why a Level Designer should care:**
A close-quarters map with zero PvP is a failed design goal. The map's 
entire identity is built around tight combat — if the storm is 
preventing that, the map needs rebalancing before players write it 
off as boring.
